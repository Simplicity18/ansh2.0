Drug Inventory and Supply Chain Tracking System

Problem Statement

Traditional drug inventory systems face mounting challenges: inaccurate stock records, lack of real-time visibility, and delayed replenishment cycles. Market Research

The global drug inventory management system market is experiencing significant growth, projected to rise from approximately $6.4 billion in 2025 to over $10 billion by 2030, driven by a CAGR exceeding 9%. This surge is fueled by the need to reduce medication errors, manage expiration, and improve operational efficiency through digital transformation. Key trends include AI-driven forecasting, cloud-based solutions, and automated dispensing cabinets. The market is dominated by centralized hospital systems, while independent pharmacies are increasingly adopting digital solutions to improve profitability. Major players include Omnicell, BD, and McKesson.

Research Gap / Innovation

Instead of relying on weekly or monthly manual inventory counts (periodic system), our solution implements a perpetual inventory model that updates instantly as medications are sold, received, or dispensed. This real-time tracking approach improves transparency, minimizes stock discrepancies, reduces expiration losses, and enhances supply chain efficiency.
